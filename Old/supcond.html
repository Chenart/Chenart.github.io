Topological Superconductor Modelling

Nov. 2015 - Present


Project Summary

Over the past year, I worked with a post-doctorate mentor to learn how to model superconductor systems using a Python software package named Kwant. Our aim is to study a particular behaviour of superconductors called topology, which, if exploited, can lead to error-free quantum computing.

Translating physical equations into code was especially challenging at the beginning, as both the physics and the coding language were new to me, but through persistence and resourceful learning I was able to develop a foundational understanding of the field.

Lattice Plot, 1D Josephson Junction

A Josephson Junction consists of a semiconductor sandwiched between two superconductors. Under certain circumstances, it exhibits topological behaviour -- the goal is to find determine what these circumstances are.

To prepare the system for computation, I initialized each point of the lattice with the system's Hamiltonian. Pictured is a plot of a lattice that is 10 atoms long, with superconducting leads attached on either side.
Energy Plot, 1D Josephson Junction

To the left is a plot of the energies of a Josephson Junction. This graph allows us to check if our system has topological behaviour, which occurs for energy = 0 eV.
Phase Diagram, 1D Josephson Junction

Another meaningful graph is to plot a phase diagram. This allows us to visualize the topological and trivial regimes of the system and determine the phase boundary.
